Get To Know "CHOLERA"

What is "Cholera"?

Cholera is an infection in the small intestine caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae, Cholera can also occur due to poor enviromental sanitation and lack of good hygiene, it is a vital disease that kills people within a period of 24 hrs. The pictures below show us how we get "Cholera"

Here are some ways in which we get Cholera

Cholera bacteria

This is bacteria viewed under a microscope which causes the disease Cholera. Scientists call the organism "Vibro-Cholerae

Eating uncovered or cold food

Flies carry harmful germs and bacteria, when food is left uncovered, flies are then attracted to sit on it and bath off their germs in food making contaminated and very dangerous for one to eat the uncovered food, so when we eat we are at high risk of getting "Cholera"

Not washing your Hands After Using the Toilet

Our hands are the ones which are mostly exposed t dirt therefore, we must wash our hands regulary with soap and running water after using the toilet and after handling any unclean objects or substances.

Unsafe Dispole of faecal sladge or Garbage at wrong places

Not disposing of waste and other anti-biodegradable substances at right locations or areas i.e at safe dumping sites
Vibrio-Cholerae Organism

Vibro cholrae is transmitted through contaminated water or the bacteria may cling to foodsthat are uncooked.

Symptoms of "Cholera"?

The primary symptoms of cholera are profuse, painless diarrhea and vomiting of clear fluid. These symptoms usually start suddenly, one to five days after ingestion of the bacteria.


Stool will be watery like rice water

The diarrhea is frequently described as "rice water" in nature and may have a fishy odour. An untreated person with cholera may produce 10 to 20 litres (3 to 5 US gal) of diarrhea a day with fatal results.

Preventive Measures

How can we prevent "Cholera"

Cleaning your toilet everyday

Proper disposal and treatment of infected fecal waste water produced by cholera victims on unvictims and all contaminated materials (e.g. clothing, bedding, etc.) are essential.
—Close Toilet pans, Clean regulary with bleach, harpic or any other household toilet cleaners.

SANITIZATION

All materials that come in contact with cholera patients should be sanitized by washing in hot water, using chlorine bleach if possible.

STERILIZATION

Hands that touch cholera patients or their clothing, bedding, etc., should be thoroughly cleaned and disinfected with chlorinated water or other effective antimicrobial agents.

WASHING HANDS WITH SOAP

Always wash your hands with soap after using the toilet, after cleaning a baby’s bottom, before handling or eating food, and before feeding a child. Hands that touch cholera patients or their clothing, bedding, etc., should be thoroughly cleaned and disinfected with chlorinated water or other effective antimicrobial agents.

Containing Human Wastes

Stay away from fruits and vegetables grown at or near ground level, irrigated with water containing human waste, or "freshened" with contaminated water, and eaten raw

Food and utensils

- Avoid eating raw food or food from sources whose cleanness you cannot verify.

- Use only boiled water from a safe water sources piped or from protected well, to drink and to wash your food.

- Wash all fruits and vegetables well with safe boiled water, and/or peel them with clean hands before eating.

- Keep boiled drinking water in a clean covered container and always keep your food covered to protect it from flies.

- Always use a clean cup to collect drinking water from the container and do not touch the water with your hands
